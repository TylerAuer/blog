If you are learning JavaScript, you might not be aware that there are multiple versions of the language. This may not seem like a big deal. Maybe you started with another language or are used to applications updating automatically. After all, your computer and phone update to new versions all the time and not much seems to change, right?

JavaScripts versions are a bigger deal. And, it's not obvious for new developers to understand why.

## Why are there different versions of JavaScript?

Programming languages evolve over time. Developers fix bugs and add new features. This a good thing, mostly. The new features especially can make your life as a developer much easier.

JavaScript was created by Brendan Eich in 1995 and was adopted by The European Computer Manufacturers Association (ECMA) in 1997 which is why it is sometimes referred to as EXMAScript
